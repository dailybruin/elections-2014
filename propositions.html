Proposition 1: Water Quality, Supply and Infrastructure Improvement Act of 2014 - Would issue bonds for $7.12 billion to finance a water quality, supply and infrastructure improvement program, including $2.7 billion for water storage projects and $725 million for water recycling and treatment projects - Would increase state bond costs with an average payment of $360 million annually over the next 40 years $ $ Proposition 2: State Budget Stabilization Account - Setting a “rainy day” fund with an annual transfer of 1.5 percent of the general fund revenues to the state budget stabilization account and personal capital gains tax revenues exceeding 8 percent of general fund revenues - The fund will be capped at 10 percent of general fund revenue, with the rest going to infrastructure. Half of the fund will go toward funding debts Proposition 45: Healthcare Insurance Rate Changes - Requires the Insurance Commissioner to approve changes to health insurance; currently the commissioner just reviews changes - Prohibits companies from considering prior insurance coverage and credit history on determining rates for automobile and homeowner’s insurance, although insurance companies generally have not used such factors Proposition 46: Drug and Alcohol Testing of Doctors and Medical Negligence Lawsuits - Requires drug and alcohol testing of doctors and reporting positive tests to the California Medical Board - Increases medical malpractice lawsuit claim limit from $250,000 to reflect the inflation; effectively to $1.1 million. Proposition 47: Criminal Sentences and Misdemeanor Penalties - Requires misdemeanor sentences for certain drug possession offenses, petty theft and writing a bad check if the amount is $950 or less - Uses the savings from doing so to support mental health and drug treatment programs, as well as programs to reduce dropouts and truancy in K-12 students Proposition 48: Referendum on Indian Gaming Compacts - Ratifies gaming compacts between the state and the North Fork Rancheria of Mono Indians and the Wiyot Tribe in the Central Valley $
